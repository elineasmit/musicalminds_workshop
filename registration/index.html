Abstract submission opens soon and details on how to submit will be published here. Deadline for abstract submission is 14 April 2023 and notifications of acceptance will be sent at the end of May.

The workshop will be a single-stream event and a select number of spoken presentations will be complemented by poster presentations. Authors will be able to select their preference for a spoken or poster presentation during submission.

There will be a student prize on offer for best presentation and poster!

Conference registration will open after notifications of acceptance have been sent out. Registration costs are expected to be roughly €70 for senior academics and €50 for (PhD) students. Lunch and coffee will be included on both days. There will be limited space available on the workshop, therefore registration will open first for accepted presenters.

Submitted abstracts are limited to 500 words or less (excluding references) and must follow the following format:
- Introduction
- Aims of the study
- Methods
- Results
- Discussion
- References (if applicable)
